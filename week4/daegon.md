# Building Agentic AI Systems

## Chapter 4 : Reflection and Introspection in Agents

### Reflection in LLM
* Definition: Ability to examine their own thought process
* Benefits: Refine & Enhance decision making
* Extend to Adaption in AI agent
    * Techniques for Reflection 
        * Traditional reasoning: pre-defined and problem-specific logic with algorithm
        * Meta-reasoing: process and control reasoning activities
        * Self-explanation: verbalizing reasoning process
            * Benefits: transparency, learning & refinement, user engagement & collaboration
    * Applications
        * Performance monitoring
        * Resource allocation

### Adaption
* Self-Modeling: mechanism for managing internal representation of goals & beliefs & Knowledge.
    * How Goals are managed (Question)
    * Knowledge vs Beliefs (Difference)
        * Certainty: Knowledge is treated as certain; beliefs have varying confidence levels
        * Revision: Knowledge is stable and rarely revised; beliefs are updated frequently as new information arrives
        * Source: Knowledge typically comes from verified sources or logical deduction; beliefs come from perception, inference, or probabilistic reasoning
        * Function: Knowledge provides the foundation for reasoning; beliefs represent the agent's current understanding that guides action
